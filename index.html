Flavours & Festivals

LivingCulture

A civilisation of 2,500 years lives on in its food, ceremonies, crafts and daily rituals.

Drag to explore

Rice & Curry

A symphony of 10 tiny bowls — dal, coconut sambol, jackfruit, dried fish and pol roti — served on banana leaf.

Esala Perahera

Kandy's ten-night festival — 100 caparisoned elephants, fire dancers and drummers parading by torchlight.

Kottu Roti

The percussive clang of metal blades chopping roti on a griddle is the unmistakable street-food soundtrack of Lanka.

Ceylon Tea

The world's finest high-grown tea. Visit a factory in Nuwara Eliya and drink it fresh-picked at 2,000m altitude.

Ayurveda

Three thousand years of herbal medicine. Restore balance at a Kandyan wellness retreat with Shirodhara and medicinal baths.

Stilt Fishermen

An iconic sight of the south coast — fishermen perched on poles in the surf, casting lines at sunset in Koggala.

Travel Tips

InsiderAdvice

01

Buy an e-visa before you travel — Sri Lanka's online portal approves in 24 hrs for most nationalities.

02

Hire a tuk-tuk for short hops and a driver-guide for multi-day circuits — it's affordable and flexible.

03

Book the Colombo–Ella train at least two weeks ahead — the observation car sells out fast.

04

Cover shoulders and knees at temples — a light cotton sarong weighs nothing and is sold everywhere.

05

LKR is the local currency; USD is accepted at most hotels. ATMs are common in towns.
